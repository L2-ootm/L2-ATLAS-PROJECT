# F5: Advanced Financial Features

## Findings

### [1] NetSuite Advanced Revenue Management (ARM) automates ASC 606 revenue recognition through revenue arrangements, revenue elements, revenue recognition rules, revenue plans, and journal entries — replacing spreadsheet-based processes.
- quote: "The Advanced Revenue Management (Essentials) feature automates revenue forecasting, recognition, reclassification, deferral, and auditing through a rule-based event handling framework."
- url: https://docs.oracle.com/en/cloud/saas/netsuite/ns-online-help/chapter_4328435538.html
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

### [2] NetSuite ARM Revenue Allocation supports fair value pricing (SSP, VSOE, ESP, TPE) to allocate revenue across performance obligations in multi-element transactions, a critical ASC 606 requirement.
- quote: "Advanced Revenue Management (Revenue Allocation) is an add-on feature to Advanced Revenue Management (Essentials). This feature supports the use of fair value pricing, range checking, and fair value formulas to allocate revenue across several performance obligations."
- url: https://docs.oracle.com/en/cloud/saas/netsuite/ns-online-help/chapter_4328435538.html
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

- quote: "Acumatica empowers you to create and manage unlimited companies within a single environment. Share charts of accounts, automate intercompany transactions, and generate consolidated reports to drive faster decision-making."

### [9] NetSuite supports multi-book accounting for revenue recognition, allowing companies to maintain different accounting treatments for different reporting requirements or jurisdictions simultaneously.
- quote: "Multi-book accounting: The company maintains different accounting treatments for different reporting requirements or jurisdictions."

### [10] The ASC 606 five-step model as implemented in ERP systems requires: (1) contract identification, (2) performance obligation identification, (3) transaction price determination including variable consideration, (4) allocation based on standalone selling price, and (5) revenue recognition at a point in time or over time — with automated schedules generated from these rules.
- quote: "The ASC 606 five-step model applies to all contracts with customers regardless of industry. Performance obligation identification determines when revenue is recognized (point in time vs. over time). Variable consideration (discounts, rebates, penalties) must be estimated and constrained."
- url: https://ecosire.com/blog/revenue-recognition-guide
- source_type: secondary
- published: 2026-03-05
- confidence: high

### [11] Sage Intacct stands out for multi-entity accounting with entity-level budgeting, allocation rules, and intercompany transactions, supporting consolidated financial reporting with automated eliminations.
- quote: "Sage Intacct stands out for multi-entity accounting built around consolidated financial reporting and strong ERP-style controls. It supports entity-level budgeting, allocation rules, and intercompany transactions so groups can close faster with fewer manual consolidations."
- url: https://www.erpresearch.com/en-us/blog/best-erp-for-multi-entity-businesses
- source_type: secondary
- published: 2026-06-30
- confidence: medium

### [12] Acumatica's Financial Management suite includes Deferred Revenue Accounting, Recurring Revenue Management, Contract Management, and Currency Management as separate, composable modules — demonstrating the modular architecture pattern for advanced financial features.
- quote: "Deferred Revenue Accounting, Recurring Revenue Management, Contract Management, Currency Management" are listed as distinct product modules under Acumatica Financial Management.
- url: https://www.acumatica.com/cloud-erp-software/financial-management/
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
