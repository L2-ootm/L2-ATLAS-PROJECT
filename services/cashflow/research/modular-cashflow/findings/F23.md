## 1. Stripe Connect Payment Flow Models

### 1.1 Direct Charges
- **Flow**: Customer pays directly to connected account; platform collects application fee
- **Use case**: SaaS platforms (Shopify storefronts, Thinkific courses)
- **Key API**:
```python
# Direct charge - customer transacts with connected account
stripe.PaymentIntent.create(
    payment_method_types=['card'],
    amount=1000,
    currency='usd',
    application_fee_amount=123  # Platform fee
)
# Pass stripe_account in header for connected account
```
- **Fund flow**: Customer → Connected Account (minus Stripe fee + application fee) → Platform gets application fee
- **Visibility**: Charge objects live on connected account, not platform

### 1.2 Destination Charges
- **Flow**: Platform collects payment, then transfers to connected account
- **Use case**: Marketplaces (Airbnb, Lyft)
- **Key API**:
```python
# Destination charge - platform collects, transfers
stripe.PaymentIntent.create(
    payment_method_types=['card'],
    amount=1000,
    currency='usd',
    application_fee_amount=123,
    transfer_data={'destination': connected_account_id}
)
```
- **Fund flow**: Customer → Platform (full amount) → Transfers to Connected Account → Platform gets back application fee
- **Alternative**: Use `transfer_data[amount]` instead of `application_fee_amount` for more control

### 1.3 Separate Charges and Transfers
- **Flow**: Platform charges customer, then makes multiple transfers to different connected accounts
- **Use case**: Payment splits between multiple parties (restaurant + delivery driver)
- **Key API**:
```python
# Step 1: Create charge on platform
stripe.PaymentIntent.create(
    payment_method_types=['card'],
    amount=10000,  # $100 total
    currency='usd',
    transfer_group='ORDER100'
)

# Step 2: Transfer to restaurant ($70)
stripe.Transfer.create(
    amount=7000,
    currency='usd',
    destination=restaurant_account_id,
    transfer_group='ORDER100'
)

# Step 3: Transfer to driver ($20)
stripe.Transfer.create(
    amount=2000,
    currency='usd',
    destination=driver_account_id,
    transfer_group='ORDER100'
)
# Platform retains: $100 - $3.20 Stripe fee - $70 - $20 = $6.80
```

## 2. Split Payment Models

### 2.1 Percentage Split
- Platform takes X% of each transaction
- Example: 15% marketplace fee on $100 = $85 to seller, $15 to platform
- API: `application_fee_amount = total * take_rate`

### 2.2 Fixed Fee Split
- Platform takes flat fee per transaction
- Example: $2.50 per booking regardless of amount
- API: `application_fee_amount = 250` (in cents)

### 2.3 Tiered Splits
- Fee varies by volume or amount thresholds
- Example: 20% for first $1K/month, 15% above $1K
- Implementation: Track seller volume, calculate fee at charge time

### 2.4 Net Revenue Split
- Platform deducts all costs, splits remaining net revenue
- Common in SaaS platforms with payment processing + hosting costs
- Requires custom calculation before setting `application_fee_amount`

## 3. Escrow Patterns

### 3.1 Holding Funds Until Condition Met
- **Pattern**: Hold in platform balance, release on event trigger
- **API approach**: Use separate charges and transfers with delayed transfer
- **Compliance**: Stripe allows holding funds for:
  - Thailand: 10 days
  - United States: 2 years
  - All other countries: 90 days
- **Best practice**: Hold funds with clear purpose and commitment to release

### 3.2 Partial Escrow
- Hold portion of payment, release rest immediately
- Example: Hold 20% for potential disputes, release 80% to seller
- Implementation: Split transfers - immediate transfer of 80%, hold 20%

### 3.3 Milestone-Based Escrow
- Release funds at defined milestones
- Example: 30% at booking, 40% at delivery, 30% after confirmation
- Implementation: Create multiple transfers with different release triggers

### 3.4 Balance States
```python
# Check connected account balance
balance = stripe.Balance.retrieve(stripe_account=connected_account_id)
# Returns: pending (not yet available), available (can be paid out now)

# Platform can also have connect_reserved balance
# Used to offset negative balances on connected accounts
```

## 4. Settlement Timing

### 4.1 Instant Settlement
- Available via Instant Payouts
- Fee: ~1% for instant access to funds
- **API**:
```python
stripe.Payout.create(
    amount=1000,
    currency='usd',
    method='instant'  # Immediate payout to debit card/bank
)
```

### 4.2 Daily Rolling
- Standard: 2-day rolling settlement in most countries
- Funds become `available` after pending period
- **API**:
```python
stripe.Payout.create(
    amount=1000,
    currency='usd',
    # method defaults to 'standard' - 2-day rolling
)
```

### 4.3 Weekly/Monthly
- Configurable via `payout_schedule` on connected accounts
- Useful for managing marketplace cash flow
- **API**:
```python
stripe.Account.modify(
    connected_account_id,
    settings={
        'payouts': {
            'schedule': {
                'interval': 'weekly',  # or 'monthly'
                'weekly_anchor': 'friday'
            }
        }
    }
)
```

### 4.4 Cash Flow Implications
| Timing | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Instant | Seller satisfaction, competitive advantage | Higher fees, cash flow pressure |
| Daily | Predictable, low cost | Seller may need instant access |
| Weekly | Simpler accounting | Delayed seller access |
| Monthly | Best for cash flow management | Seller dissatisfaction risk |

## 5. Seller Onboarding (KYC/KYB)

### 5.1 Identity Verification Requirements
**For Individuals (Sole Proprietors)**:
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Last 4 SSN (US) or full SSN for tax reporting
- Government-issued ID document
- Proof of address (if needed)

**For Companies**:
- Legal business name
- Employer Identification Number (EIN) or equivalent
- Business structure (LLC, Corp, etc.)
- Company address
- Business URL and description
- Tax ID (CNPJ in Brazil)

### 5.2 Brazilian Requirements (L2 ATLAS Context)
- **KYC**: CPF (Cadastro de Pessoa Física) for individuals
- **KYB**: CNPJ (Cadastro Nacional da Pessoa Jurídica) for companies
- **Proof of Authorization (PoA)**: Representative must be authorized
- **UBO Verification**: Ultimate Beneficial Owners with >25% ownership
- **Liveness Check**: Required for Brazilian accounts
- **Financial Capacity**: Monthly estimated revenue required

### 5.3 Stripe Onboarding Flows
```python
# Create connected account
account = stripe.Account.create(
    type='express',  # or 'standard', 'custom'
    country='BR',
    email='seller@example.com',
    capabilities={
        'card_payments': {'requested': True},
        'transfers': {'requested': True}
    },
    business_type='individual',
    individual={
        'first_name': 'João',
        'last_name': 'Santos',
        'dob': {'day': 15, 'month': 6, 'year': 1990},
        'id_number': '123.456.789-00'  # CPF
    }
)

# Generate onboarding link
account_link = stripe.AccountLink.create(
    account=account.id,
    refresh_url='https://example.com/reauth',
    return_url='https://example.com/return',
    type='account_onboarding'
)
```

### 5.4 Verification Thresholds
- **US (Individuals)**: EIN + SSN last 4 required at $600 lifetime or 30 days
- **US (Companies)**: EIN required at $3K-$10K threshold
- **Brazil**: CPF/CNPJ verified against government registry
- **Capabilities disabled** if verification not completed at threshold

## 6. Marketplace Financial Reporting

### 6.1 Key Metrics
- **GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)**: Total sales volume processed
- **Net Revenue**: Platform fees collected after refunds/chargebacks
- **Take Rate**: Net Revenue / GMV (typically 10-30%)
- **Seller Payouts**: Total funds transferred to sellers
- **Refund Rate**: Refunds / GMV

### 6.2 Application Fee Reporting
```python
# Retrieve application fees
fees = stripe.ApplicationFee.list(
    limit=100,
    created={'gte': start_timestamp, 'lte': end_timestamp}
)

# Each fee has:
# - amount: Platform fee collected
# - charge: Associated charge ID
# - connected_account: Seller who paid the fee
# - created: Timestamp
```

### 6.3 Balance Transaction Reporting
```python
# Get detailed fee breakdown
balance_txn = stripe.BalanceTransaction.retrieve(charge.balance_transaction)
# Includes:
# - fee: Stripe processing fee
# - fee_details: Breakdown of all fees
# - net: Amount after all fees
```

## 7. Multi-Sided Marketplace Accounting

### 7.1 Revenue Recognition
```python
# When charge is created:
# Dr. Accounts Receivable (or Stripe Balance)  $100.00
#    Cr. Deferred Revenue (Escrow)              $85.00
#    Cr. Commission Revenue (Platform Fee)      $15.00

# When funds are released to seller:
# Dr. Deferred Revenue (Escrow)                 $85.00
#    Cr. Cash/Bank                              $85.00

# When Stripe fee is deducted:
# Dr. Payment Processing Expense                $3.20
#    Cr. Cash/Bank                              $3.20
```

### 7.2 Deferred Revenue for Escrowed Funds
- Funds held in escrow = liability on platform balance sheet
- Recognize as revenue only when earned (service completed, delivery confirmed)
- **Stripe Balance State**:
  - `pending`: Not yet available (matches "held in escrow")
  - `available`: Ready for payout (matches "earned")

### 7.3 Chargeback and Dispute Handling
```python
# When dispute occurs:
# 1. Stripe debits platform account
# 2. Platform must recover from seller (reverse transfer)

# Reverse transfer to recover disputed funds
stripe.TransferReversal.create(
    transfer=transfer_id,
    amount=disputed_amount
)

# Or if auto-debit enabled:
# Stripe attempts to debit seller's bank account
# Set debit_negative_balances: true on account
```

### 7.4 Negative Balance Management
- If seller's balance goes negative (refund > earnings):
  - Platform responsible: `controller.losses.payments = 'application'`
  - Stripe responsible: `controller.losses.payments = 'stripe'`
- Platform reserve: Stripe holds portion of platform's available balance
- Auto-debit: Can automatically debit seller's bank for negative balance

## 8. International Marketplaces

### 8.1 Cross-Border Payment Support
**Stripe Connect Supported Regions**:
- US, Canada, UK, EEA, Switzerland (cross-border transfers)
- Brazil (requires `source_transaction` for transfers)
- Other countries: Same-region only

### 8.2 Currency Conversion
```python
# Multi-currency charges
stripe.PaymentIntent.create(
    amount=10000,  # Amount in charge currency
    currency='eur',  # Customer pays in EUR
    transfer_data={
        'destination': connected_account_id,
        'amount': 8500  # Converted amount to seller's currency
    }
)

# Or use adaptive pricing
# Stripe automatically converts to seller's settlement currency
```

### 8.3 Withholding Tax Considerations
- **US**: 1099-K reporting for sellers with >$20K/200 transactions
- **Brazil**: CPF/CNPJ required, IRPF/IRPJ withholding
- **EU**: VAT MOSS for digital services
- **Implementation**: Collect tax forms during onboarding, report annually

### 8.4 Transfer Pricing Between Entities
- Platform entity in one country, sellers in another
- **Stripe Approach**: Use `on_behalf_of` parameter
  - Determines settlement merchant
  - Affects statement descriptor, fees, settlement currency
  - Must match connected account's country for cross-border

```python
# Cross-border destination charge with on_behalf_of
stripe.PaymentIntent.create(
    amount=1000,
    currency='usd',
    application_fee_amount=123,
    transfer_data={'destination': seller_account_id},
    on_behalf_of=seller_account_id  # Seller is settlement merchant
)
```

## 9. Implementation Recommendations for L2 ATLAS

### 9.1 Recommended Architecture
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   L2 ATLAS Platform                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Payment Service Layer                              │
│  ├── Stripe Connect Integration                     │
│  │   ├── Destination Charges (primary flow)         │
│  │   ├── Separate Charges + Transfers (multi-party) │
│  │   └── Direct Charges (SaaS mode)                │
│  ├── Escrow Manager                                 │
│  │   ├── Balance Tracking (pending/available)       │
│  │   ├── Release Conditions (milestone-based)       │
│  │   └── Dispute Handling (transfer reversals)      │
│  └── Settlement Engine                              │
│      ├── Instant Payouts (seller opt-in)            │
│      ├── Daily Rolling (default)                    │
│      └── Custom Schedules (configurable)            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Onboarding Service                                 │
│  ├── KYC/KYB Verification (Stripe Identity)         │
│  ├── Tax Form Collection (W-9/W-8BEN, CPF/CNPJ)    │
│  └── Account Link Generation                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Reporting Service                                  │
│  ├── GMV/Take Rate Metrics                          │
│  ├── Application Fee Reconciliation                 │
│  └── Tax Reporting (1099-K, local equivalents)      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 9.2 Key Design Decisions
1. **Primary Flow**: Destination charges for simplicity, separate charges for multi-party
2. **Escrow**: Use pending balance + conditional transfers
3. **Settlement**: Default to daily rolling, offer instant as premium
4. **Onboarding**: Stripe Connect Onboarding (hosted) for compliance
5. **Accounting**: Separate deferred revenue from commission revenue

### 9.3 Brazil-Specific Considerations
- Mandatory CPF/CNPJ verification against Receita Federal
- Liveness check required for all connected accounts
- Proof of Authorization (PoA) for non-individual accounts
- UBO verification with >25% ownership threshold
- Financial capacity estimation required at onboarding

## 12. Alternative Platforms Comparison

### Stripe Connect vs Adyen vs PayPal

| Feature | Stripe Connect | Adyen | PayPal for Marketplaces |
|---------|---------------|-------|------------------------|
| Split Models | Direct, Destination, Separate | Split payments, separate transfers | Parallel payments |
| Escrow | Balance-based (pending/available) | Held in Adyen balance | Limited escrow support |
| Onboarding | Hosted, Embedded, Custom | Hosted KYC | PayPal Commerce Platform |
| Settlement | Instant, Daily, Weekly, Monthly | Configurable | PayPal balance + Payouts |
| Countries | 46+ countries | 100+ countries | 200+ markets |
| API Maturity | Excellent | Good | Moderate |

**Recommendation for L2 ATLAS**: Stripe Connect for primary integration (best API, documentation, and Brazil support), with Adyen as fallback for regions where Stripe has limitations.

## 13. Key Findings Summary

1. **Stripe Connect offers 3 distinct payment flow models**, each suited for different marketplace architectures
2. **Escrow is balance-state based** (pending vs available), not a separate product
3. **Brazil requires strict KYC/KYB** with CPF/CNPJ verification, liveness checks, and UBO verification
4. **Settlement timing is configurable** from instant (premium) to monthly
5. **Accounting requires separate recognition** of deferred revenue (escrow) vs commission revenue
6. **Cross-border payments** require careful handling of `on_behalf_of` and settlement merchants
7. **Dispute handling** requires transfer reversals and potential re-transfers
8. **Application fees** are the primary mechanism for platform revenue capture
