## Finding 1: IFRS 15 Five-Step Revenue Model — Complete Framework

**Standard reference**: IFRS 15 *Revenue from Contracts with Customers* (IASB, May 2014, effective 1 Jan 2018)

The five-step model is the core revenue recognition framework:

| Step | Standard Reference | Key Paragraphs |
|------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1. Identify the contract | IFRS 15.9-16 | Contract approval, identifiable rights, payment terms, commercial substance, collectability |
| 2. Identify performance obligations | IFRS 15.22-30 | Distinct goods/services per IFRS 15.27 (capable of being distinct + separately identifiable) |
| 3. Determine transaction price | IFRS 15.47-72 | Fixed/variable consideration, significant financing component, non-cash consideration |
| 4. Allocate transaction price | IFRS 15.73-86 | Relative standalone selling price (SSP) allocation |
| 5. Recognize revenue | IFRS 15.31-45 | Point-in-time or over-time, measure of progress |

**Implementation pattern**: Each step should be a distinct processing stage in the cashflow engine. Step 1 (contract) creates a `ContractContext`; Step 2 generates `PerformanceObligation[]`; Step 3 computes `TransactionPrice`; Step 4 produces `AllocationResult[]`; Step 5 executes `RevenueRecognitionEvent`.

## Finding 2: IFRS 15 Contract Identification Criteria (IFRS 15.9)

A contract exists when ALL of the following are met:

1. Both parties have approved the contract and are committed to perform (IFRS 15.9(a))
2. Each party's rights regarding goods/services are identifiable (IFRS 15.9(b))
3. Payment terms are identifiable (IFRS 15.9(c))
4. Contract has commercial substance (IFRS 15.9(d))
5. It is probable the entity will collect the consideration (IFRS 15.9(e))

**Additional considerations**: IFRS 15.12-13 — combination of contracts (same customer, negotiated as package, consideration depends on other contracts). IFRS 15.14-16 — contract modifications (prospective, cumulative catch-up, or separate contract depending on distinct goods/services and pricing).

**Implementation pattern**: The platform must store contract metadata as a structured object with fields matching each criterion. Contract modifications should be modeled as versioned sub-records with a `ModificationType` enum: `SEPARATE_CONTRACT`, `PROSPECTIVE`, `CUMULATIVE_CATCH_UP`.

## Finding 3: IFRS 15 Performance Obligation Identification (IFRS 15.22-30)

A good or service is **distinct** (IFRS 15.27) when:

1. **Capable of being distinct** (IFRS 15.27(a)): The customer can benefit from the good/service on its own or with readily available resources.
2. **Separately identifiable** (IFRS 15.27(b)): The promise to transfer is separately identifiable from other promises in the contract. Factors include (IFRS 15.29):
   - Entity does not provide a significant integration service
   - Good/service does not significantly modify or customize another
   - Goods/services are not highly interdependent or interrelated

**Variable consideration** (IFRS 15.50-55):
- **Most likely amount** method: best estimate for binary outcomes (IFRS 15.53)
- **Expected value** method: probability-weighted for distributions (IFRS 15.53)
- **Constraint**: Include variable consideration only to the extent a significant reversal is not probable (IFRS 15.56-58)

**Implementation pattern**: Model `PerformanceObligation` with a `Distinct` boolean and `DistinctRationale` enum. Variable consideration requires a `ConstraintAssessment` object that tracks the maximum amount not subject to significant reversal.

## Finding 4: IFRS 15 SSP Allocation Methods (IFRS 15.77-79)

Three methods for estimating standalone selling price:

| Method | Standard Reference | When to Use |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Adjusted market assessment** | IFRS 15.79(a) | Entity considers market prices and adjusts for costs/margins |
| **Expected cost plus margin** | IFRS 15.79(b) | Entity forecasts expected costs and adds appropriate margin |
| **Residual approach** | IFRS 15.79(c) | SSP is highly variable or uncertain; price is established by entity with no discernible SS; or SSP is variable |

**Residual approach restrictions** (IFRS 15.79(c)): Only applicable when the SSP is highly variable (IFRS 15.84(a)) or highly uncertain (IFRS 15.84(b)).

**Allocation to discounts** (IFRS 15.81-82): A discount should be allocated entirely to one or more performance obligations only if observable evidence indicates the discount relates to those specific obligations.

**Allocation to variable consideration** (IFRS 15.85-86): Variable consideration may be allocated to a specific performance obligation only if criteria in IFRS 15.85-86 are met (related to same contract, same distinct pattern of transfer).

**Implementation pattern**: Create an `SSPEstimationEngine` with strategy pattern for each method. Store SSP estimates per product/service SKU. Discount allocation requires a `DiscountAllocationPolicy` that specifies whether discount is pro-rata or targeted.

## Finding 5: IFRS 15 Revenue Recognition Criteria (IFRS 15.31-45)

**Point-in-time recognition** (IFRS 15.38): Revenue recognized when control transfers. Indicators of control transfer (IFRS 15.39):
- Entity has present right to payment
- Customer has legal title
- Entity has transferred physical possession
- Customer has significant risks and rewards of ownership

**Over-time recognition** (IFRS 15.35): Recognized over time when ANY of three criteria met:
1. Customer simultaneously receives and consumes benefits (IFRS 15.35(a))
2. Entity's performance creates/enhances an asset controlled by customer (IFRS 15.35(b))
3. Entity's performance does not create an asset with alternative use AND entity has enforceable right to payment for performance completed to date (IFRS 15.35(c))

**Measure of progress** (IFRS 15.41-45):
- **Output methods**: Units delivered, milestones reached, surveys (IFRS 15.B15-B19)
- **Input methods**: Costs incurred, time elapsed, resources consumed (IFRS 15.B20-B30)

**Implementation pattern**: Model `RecognitionCriteria` with a discriminator: `POINT_IN_TIME` or `OVER_TIME`. For over-time, include `ProgressMethod` (OUTPUT or INPUT) and `ProgressMeasure` (percentage, milestone). A `RevenueSchedule` is generated that amortizes the allocated amount over the performance period.

## Finding 6: IFRS 16 Lessee Accounting Model

**Standard reference**: IFRS 16 *Leases* (IASB, Jan 2016, effective 1 Jan 2019)

IFRS 16 replaces the operating/finance lease classification for lessees with a single model. All leases > 12 months (unless low-value) require:

1. **Right-of-use (ROU) asset** recognition (IFRS 16.23-30):
   - Initial measurement = lease liability + initial direct costs + pre-paid lease payments - lease incentives received + estimated restoration costs (IFRS 16.24)
   - Subsequent: cost model or revaluation model (IFRS 16.31-36)

2. **Lease liability** calculation (IFRS 16.26):
   - PV of lease payments not yet paid (IFRS 16.26)
   - Lease payments include: fixed payments, variable payments based on index/rate, amounts expected to be paid under residual value guarantees, exercise price of purchase option (if reasonably certain), penalties for termination (if lease term reflects entity will exercise option)
   - Discount rate: implicit rate if determinable, otherwise lessee's incremental borrowing rate (IFRS 16.26)

3. **Subsequent measurement** (IFRS 16.32-35):
   - Lease liability: accrete interest using effective interest method; reduce by lease payments made
   - ROU asset: depreciated on straight-line basis (or another systematic basis) over shorter of useful life or lease term (unless ownership transfers/purchase option reasonably certain)

**Short-term lease exemption** (IFRS 16.5-8): Leases with term ≤ 12 months (no purchase option reasonably certain) may be expensed on straight-line basis. Election on lease-by-lease basis.

**Low-value asset exemption** (IFRS 16.5-8): Leases of underlying assets that are new or used for the first time AND have low value when new. Typical threshold: ~$5,000 USD. Includes tablets, small items of office furniture, telephones.

**Implementation pattern**: Create a `LeaseContract` model with fields: `Term`, `Payments[]`, `DiscountRate`, `PurchaseOption`, `ResidualGuarantee`, `LowValueFlag`, `ShortTermFlag`. A `LeaseEngine` computes ROU asset and liability at inception, then generates amortization schedule. Subsequent measurement recalculates liability on remeasurement events.

## Finding 7: IFRS 16 Lease Modifications

**Lease modification** (IFRS 16.44-46): A change in scope or consideration not included in the original contract.

| Modification Type | Treatment | Standard Reference |
|------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Not a separate lease | Adjust lease liability at modification date using revised discount rate; proportionally adjust ROU asset | IFRS 16.44-45 |
| Separate lease (scope increase + standalone price) | Account for as new lease from modification date | IFRS 16.44(a) |
| Decrease in scope | Reduce ROU asset proportionally; gain/loss recognized in P&L | IFRS 16.44(b) |

**Remeasurement events**: Changes in lease term, purchase option certainty, or amounts under residual value guarantees trigger remeasurement of lease liability using revised discount rate (IFRS 16.36-40).

**Sale and leaseback** (IFRS 16.98-106): After the 2022 amendment, the lease liability is measured at fair value of the right-of-use asset (not at the proportion of original liability), ensuring no gain/loss recognition on the sale portion.

**Implementation pattern**: Model `LeaseModification` as an event that produces a `ModificationResult` containing: `Type` (SEPARATE, SCOPE_INCREASE, SCOPE_DECREASE), `RevisedLiability`, `ROUAdjustment`, `GainLoss`. The modification engine must recalculate the amortization schedule from the modification date.

## Finding 8: IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts — Building Blocks Approach

**Standard reference**: IFRS 17 *Insurance Contracts* (IASB, May 2017, effective 1 Jan 2023)

IFRS 17 has three measurement models:

### General Measurement Model (GMM) — IFRS 17.44-55

Insurance contract liability at initial recognition = **Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF)** + **Contractual Service Margin (CSM)**

**Fulfilment Cash Flows** (IFRS 17.37-43):
- Future cash flows (premiums, claims, expenses) estimated using current, unbiased, probability-weighted amounts
- Adjusted for non-financial risk (risk adjustment for non-financial risk, IFRS 17.37(b))
- Discounted using current rates (IFRS 17.BC162-B163)

**CSM** (IFRS 17.44-49):
- Represents unearned profit at initial recognition
- CSM = -FCF at initial recognition (if FCF is a liability)
- Prevents day-one profit recognition
- Released to profit/loss over coverage units (IFRS 17.43-46)

### Premium Allocation Approach (PAA) — IFRS 17.53-64

Simplified method eligible when:
- PAA would not differ materially from GMM (IFRS 17.53(a))
- Coverage period is one year or less (IFRS 17.53(b))

Under PAA:
- Insurance revenue = premiums received/releases of liability for remaining coverage
- Insurance service expenses = incurred claims + expenses
- No CSM; profit recognized proportionally to coverage

### Variable Fee Approach (VFA) — IFRS 17.B105-B111

For contracts with direct participation features where the entity's share of underlying item performance constitutes a variable fee. CSM is adjusted for changes in financial risk (unlike GMM where only non-financial risk adjustments go to CSM).

**Implementation pattern**: Create `InsuranceContractGroup` with `MeasurementModel` enum (GMM, PAA, VFA). Each model requires different data: GMM needs actuarial assumptions, VFA needs underlying item performance data, PAA needs premium schedule. The CSM amortization engine must track coverage units per group.

## Finding 9: IFRS 17 Contractual Service Margin (CSM) Mechanics

**Initial CSM** (IFRS 17.44): CSM at initial recognition = present value of future unearned profit.

**Subsequent CSM movements** (IFRS 17.44-49):

| Movement | Description | Affects CSM? |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Current service margin | Profit for coverage provided in period | Yes (released) |
| Past service margin | Experience adjustments for past service | Yes (adjust CSM) |
| Future service margin changes | Changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to future service | Yes (adjust CSM) |
| Insurance finance income/expenses | Change in FCF due to passage of time | No (to OCI or P&L per policy) |
| Currency differences | Exchange rate changes | No (to OCI) |
| Losses on onerous contracts | If FCF exceeds CSM → immediate loss recognition | N/A (CSM = 0, loss to P&L) |

**Coverage units** (IFRS 17.B87-B90): CSM released in proportion to coverage units provided in current and future periods. Coverage units typically based on expected number of claims, expected claims amounts, or time-based measures.

**Implementation pattern**: CSM is a running balance per contract group. Each reporting period requires: (1) recalculate FCF, (2) split FCF changes into current/past/future, (3) release CSM based on coverage units, (4) check for onerous contract test. Model as a state machine: `ACTIVE` → `ONEROUS` (irreversible for that group).

## Finding 10: ASC 606 vs IFRS 15 — Key Differences

**ASC 606** is the US GAAP equivalent of IFRS 15, developed jointly. While substantially converged, key differences exist:

| Topic | IFRS 15 | ASC 606 | Impact |
|-------|---------|---------|--------|
| **Principal vs Agent** | IFRS 15.B34-B38: control indicators | ASC 606-10-55-36 to 55-40: slightly different indicators emphasis | Software reseller arrangements may classify differently |
| **Licensing revenue** | IFRS 15.B55-B61: right to access vs right to use | ASC 606-10-55-54 to 55-58: substantially similar but different wording on "functional" vs "symbolic" IP | Practical application can differ for digital content |
| **Contract costs** | IFRS 15.91-104: asset for costs to obtain/fulfill | ASC 340-40-25-1 to 25-4: similar but explicit scope exception for leases | Consistent outcome, minor scope differences |
| **Nonrefundable upfront fees** | IFRS 15.B49-B50: generally part of SSP | ASC 606-10-55-42 to 55-45: similar treatment but different practical expedients | Telecom activations, club memberships |
| **Impairment of contract assets** | IFRS 15.107-108: loss allowance per IFRS 9 | ASC 606-10-32-4: similar | Consistent |
| **Significant financing component** | Practical expedient: ≤12 months = no adjustment | Practical expedient: same | Consistent |
| **Disclosure** | IFRS 15.110-129 | ASC 606-10-50: largely parallel | Some disaggregation differences |

**Implementation pattern**: The platform should store transactions tagged with `AccountingStandard` (IFRS15, ASC606). A `StandardDifferentialEngine` maps common differences, particularly for principal/agent determination and licensing revenue. Contract cost capitalization rules are nearly identical and can share a single implementation.

## Finding 11: Multi-GAAP Reporting Architecture

**Parallel books pattern**: To maintain multiple GAAP views (local + IFRS + US GAAP):

1. **Base ledger**: Record transactions in local GAAP (statutory requirements of jurisdiction)
2. **Adjustment layer**: IFRS and US GAAP adjustments are recorded as delta entries against the base ledger
3. **Reporting views**: Each GAAP view = base ledger + relevant adjustment layer

**Key adjustment categories**:

| Adjustment Type | Example Standards | Typical Delta |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Lease classification | IFRS 16 vs ASC 842 | Operating vs finance lease split |
| Revenue timing | IFRS 15 vs ASC 606 | Minor timing differences |
| Inventory valuation | IAS 2 vs ASC 330 (LIFO allowed in US GAAP) | Cost method differences |
| Development costs | IAS 38 (capitalize if criteria met) vs ASC 730 (expense) | Capitalization vs expense |
| Revaluation | IAS 16 (revaluation model) vs ASC 360 (not allowed) | Asset carrying values |
| Impairment | IAS 36 (reversal allowed) vs ASC 350/360 (no reversal) | Goodwill/asset values |

**Intercompany elimination** (IAS 27 / IFRS 10 / ASC 810):
- Eliminate intercompany revenues, expenses, assets, liabilities
- Eliminate unrealized profits on intercompany transactions
- Currency differences in elimination entries

**Translation adjustments** (IAS 21 / ASC 830):
- Functional currency determination (IAS 21.9-14)
- Closing rate method for balance sheet; average rate for income statement
- Translation differences to OCI (IAS 21.39) — not recycled to P&L on disposal
- US GAAP: same basic approach but CTA treatment on disposal differs (ASC 830-10-45)

**Implementation pattern**: Model the ledger as a `MultiGAAPLedger` with:
- `Transaction`: base record with `LocalGAAP` tag
- `AdjustmentEntry`: delta entries tagged with `TargetStandard` (IFRS, US_GAAP, LOCAL)
- `ReportingView`: configuration specifying which base + adjustments to aggregate
- `ConsolidationEngine`: handles intercompany elimination and translation across all views

## Finding 12: Chart of Accounts Design Patterns

### Universal COA Architecture

The platform must support a **mapping layer** between national COAs and a universal internal COA:

```
National COA (SKR03/PCG/ECF) → Mapping Table → Universal Internal COA → Reporting COA
```

### National COA Mappings

| Country | COA Standard | Structure | Key Characteristics |
|---------|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Germany** | SKR03 (4,900 accounts) | 4-digit, 9 groups (1=assets, 2=liabilities, 3=income, 4=expense, 5-9=subcategories) | Dual-column system: SKR03 for accrual, SKR04 for tax-oriented |
| **Germany** | SKR04 (3,700 accounts) | 3-digit, similar grouping | More granular expense/revenue classification |
| **France** | PCG (Plan Comptable Général) | 3-digit class (1-7) + 2-digit subaccount | Class 1=capital, 2=assets, 3=stock, 4=third parties, 5=financial, 6=expenses, 7=income |
| **Brazil** | ECF (Escrituração Contábil Fiscal) | RFB layout with specific field codes | Must map to SPED (Sistema Público de Escrituração Digital) XML; CTB (Comissão de Tributação Bancária) structure |
| **Brazil** | CTB (Código de Tributação Bancário) | 5-digit (group 2 digits + subgroup 3 digits) | Mandatory for financial institutions |
| **UK** | FRS 102 / UK GAAP | No mandated structure; typically 4-5 digits | Flexible; Companies House requirements |
| **US** | No mandated COA | Varies; industry templates exist | FASB ASC topical alignment for reporting |
| **China** | Enterprise Accounting Standards (CAS) | 4-digit base per MOF regulations | Mandatory account names for statutory filings |

### COA Hierarchy Design

A universal COA should have 4-5 levels:

```
Level 1: Financial Statement Category (Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expenses)
Level 2: Sub-category (Current Assets, Non-current Assets, etc.)
Level 3: Account Group (Cash, Receivables, Inventory)
Level 4: Account (Bank Account - USD, Bank Account - EUR)
Level 5: Sub-account (Department code, Cost center, Project code)
```

**Implementation pattern**:

```python
class COAAccount:
    id: str                    # Universal internal ID
    code: str                  # Universal code (e.g., "1100-001")
    name: str
    level: int                 # Hierarchy level
    parent_id: str | None
    account_type: AccountType  # ASSET, LIABILITY, EQUITY, REVENUE, EXPENSE
    
class COAMapping:
    source_system: str         # "SKR03", "PCG", "ECF", "CTB"
    source_code: str           # National COA code
    target_account_id: str     # Universal account ID
    direction: MappingDirection # STANDARD, REVERSE (for contra accounts)
    effective_date: date
```

**Key design decisions**:

1. **Store both native and universal codes**: Never lose the original national classification. The mapping table should be versioned (effective dates) since COA standards change.

2. **Handle multi-currency accounts at COA level**: Some COAs embed currency in the account code (e.g., Brazil ECF). The universal COA should separate currency from account classification.

3. **Support tagging over rigid hierarchies**: Use a tag/label system (e.g., `department:finance`, `cost_center:HQ`, `project:ATLAS`) for reporting flexibility instead of encoding everything in the account code.

4. **IC (Intercompany) accounts**: Standardize intercompany account ranges (e.g., 9xxx) that are eliminated during consolidation regardless of national COA.

5. **Tax-relevant accounts**: Flag accounts that map to specific tax return line items. This varies by country: Brazil SPED, German Anlage AUR, French liasse fiscale.

## Finding 13: Revenue Recognition for SaaS/Subscription Platforms

Given the cashflow platform context, SaaS-specific revenue patterns are critical:

**Annual prepaid subscription** (IFRS 15):
- One performance obligation: access to software over subscription period
- Revenue recognized over time (IFRS 15.35(a): customer simultaneously receives and consumes benefits)
- SSP = annual subscription price
- No significant financing component if paid upfront

**Implementation + license bundles**:
- Implementation = distinct if customer can benefit without customization (IFRS 15.27)
- If distinct → separate performance obligations → allocate SSP
- If not distinct (significant customization) → single obligation → recognize over longer period

**Usage-based pricing** (variable consideration):
- Transaction price includes variable component (per-user, per-API-call)
- Estimate using expected value (IFRS 15.53) or most likely amount
- Apply constraint (IFRS 15.56-58): recognize only when highly probable of no significant reversal

**Platform fees with usage**:
- Fixed platform fee = distinct obligation (access)
- Usage fees = variable consideration tied to access obligation
- Allocate based on relative SSP if usage can be separately estimated

## Finding 14: Cross-Standard Implementation Architecture

**Recommended data model for multi-standard support**:

```
Transaction
├── GAAPAdjustment[]
│   ├── standard: GAAPStandard (IFRS, US_GAAP, LOCAL)
│   ├── adjustment_type: AdjustmentType
│   ├── amount: Decimal
│   └── rationale: str
├── COAMapping[]
│   ├── source_standard: str
│   ├── source_account: str
│   └── target_account: str
└── ReportingView[]
    ├── name: str
    ├── included_standards: GAAPStandard[]
    └── consolidation_rules: ConsolidationRule[]
```

**Key interfaces**:

```typescript
interface RevenueRecognitionStandard {
    identifyContract(contract: RawContract): Contract
    identifyObligations(contract: Contract): PerformanceObligation[]
    determinePrice(contract: Contract, obligations: PerformanceObligation[]): TransactionPrice
    allocatePrice(price: TransactionPrice, obligations: PerformanceObligation[]): AllocationResult[]
    recognizeRevenue(allocation: AllocationResult, context: RecognitionContext): RevenueEvent[]
}

interface LeaseAccountingStandard {
    calculateInitialLiability(lease: LeaseTerms): LeaseLiability
    calculateROUAsset(liability: LeaseLiability, costs: LeaseCosts): ROUAsset
    subsequentMeasurement(asset: ROUAsset, liability: LeaseLiability, period: Period): LeaseMeasurement
    handleModification(lease: LeaseState, modification: LeaseModification): ModificationResult
}

interface InsuranceAccountingStandard {
    calculateFCF(group: ContractGroup, assumptions: ActuarialAssumptions): FulfilmentCashFlows
    calculateCSM(group: ContractGroup, fcf: FulfilmentCashFlows): CSM
    amortizeCSM(csm: CSM, coverageUnits: CoverageUnits): CSMAmortization
    lossComponentTest(group: ContractGroup): LossAssessment
}
```

## Finding 15: Regulatory Filing Considerations

Different jurisdictions require specific outputs:

| Jurisdiction | Filing System | Format | Key Requirements |
|-------------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| Brazil | SPED (ECF, ECD, EFD) | XML | Mandatory digital bookkeeping; specific COA mapping to CTB; DCTF for tax |
| Germany | HGB filings | XBRL | SKR03/04 mandatory for commercial register; E-Bilanz digital filing |
| France | Liasses fiscales | XBRL | PCG-based; mandatory digital submission; bilan comptable format |
| US | SEC EDGAR | XBRL/iXBRL | US GAAP taxonomy; IFRS taxonomy for foreign private issuers |
| International | IFRS Foundation | iXBRL | IFRS taxonomy; IFRS 18 (2024) new presentation requirements |

**IFRS 18 impact** (effective 2027): IFRS 18 *Presentation and Disclosure in Financial Statements* replaces IAS 1 and introduces:
- Defined management-defined performance measures (MPMs) in statement of profit or loss
- Enhanced disaggregation requirements
- New subtotals on the income statement

**Implementation implication**: The platform must generate jurisdiction-specific output formats from the universal COA and GAAP adjustment layers.

## Summary

This research covers the complete landscape of accounting standards a universal cashflow platform must support. The key architectural insight is that **all standards share common data patterns** (contracts, assets, liabilities, revenue/expense recognition) but diverge on **measurement rules, recognition timing, and classification**. A well-designed platform separates:

1. **Transaction capture** (standard-agnostic)
2. **Measurement engine** (standard-specific logic via strategy pattern)
3. **Multi-GAAP adjustment layer** (delta entries for each standard)
4. **Reporting output** (jurisdiction-specific formats)

This separation ensures that adding a new accounting standard or national COA requires only new strategy implementations and mapping tables, not core engine changes.
