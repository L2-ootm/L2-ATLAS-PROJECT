## 1. Standard Financial Statements

### 1.1 Balance Sheet (Statement of Financial Position)

**IFRS Format (IAS 1):**
- Ordered by liquidity: least liquid (land/buildings) at top, most liquid (cash) at bottom
- Sections: Non-current Assets → Current Assets → Current Liabilities → Non-current Liabilities → Equity
- Requires: Share capital reconciliation, treasury shares, rights/preferences of shares, reserve descriptions

**US GAAP Format:**
- Current items first (reverse liquidity order)
- Sections: Current Assets → Non-current Assets → Current Liabilities → Long-term Liabilities → Equity

**Key Structure (IFRS consolidated):**
```
ASSETS
  Non-Current Assets
    PPE (net of depreciation)
    Goodwill
    Intangible Assets (net of amortization)
    Investments in Associates/JVs
    Deferred Tax Assets
  Current Assets
    Inventories
    Prepaid Expenses
    Trade Receivables (net of allowances)
    Cash and Cash Equivalents
TOTAL ASSETS

LIABILITIES & EQUITY
  Current Liabilities
    Accounts Payable
    Current Tax Payable
    Short-term Provisions
  Non-current Liabilities
    Loans Payable
    Deferred Tax Liabilities
    Pension Obligations
  Equity
    Share Capital
    Share Premium
    Retained Earnings
    Revaluation Reserve
    Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income
    Non-Controlling Interest
```

### 1.2 Income Statement (Statement of Profit or Loss)

**Single-Step Method:**
- Groups all revenues together, subtracts all expenses
- Simpler presentation, no subtotals like gross profit
```
Revenue                          $X
Expenses:
  Cost of goods sold             (X)
  SG&A                          (X)
  Depreciation                   (X)
  Interest                       (X)
  Tax                            (X)
  Total Expenses                (X)
Net Income                       $X
```

**Multi-Step Method (IFRS/US GAAP):**
- Multiple subtotals: Gross Profit → Operating Income → Income Before Tax → Net Income
```
Revenue                          $X
Cost of Sales                   (X)
Gross Profit                     $X
Distribution Costs              (X)
Administrative Expenses         (X)
Operating Profit (EBIT)          $X
Finance Costs                   (X)
Share of Associates' Profit      X
Profit Before Tax                $X
Income Tax Expense              (X)
Net Income                       $X
```

**IFRS Requirements (IAS 1.82):**
1. Revenue
2. Finance costs
3. Share of profit of associates/JVs
4. Tax expense
5. Discontinued operations (single post-tax amount)
6. Profit or loss
7. Each component of OCI
8. Share of OCI of associates
9. Total comprehensive income

**IFRS vs US GAAP Differences:**
- R&D: IFRS allows capitalization of development costs; US GAAP expenses immediately
- Inventory: IFRS prohibits LIFO; US GAAP allows LIFO
- Impairment: IFRS allows reversal; US GAAP forbids reversal
- Extraordinary items: Banned under both (US GAAP since ASU 2015-01)

### 1.3 Cash Flow Statement (Statement of Cash Flows)

**Standard (IAS 7):** Three sections:
1. **Operating Activities** - production, sales, delivery, collecting payments
2. **Investing Activities** - asset purchases/sales, loans to suppliers, M&A
3. **Financing Activities** - dividends paid, stock buybacks, borrowings, debt repayment

**Direct Method (IAS 7 recommended, rare in practice):**
```
Cash receipts from customers        $9,500
Cash paid to suppliers/employees   (2,000)
Cash generated from operations      $7,500
Interest paid                      (2,000)
Income taxes paid                  (3,000)
Net cash from operating activities  $2,500
```

**Indirect Method (universally used):**
```
Net Income                          $100
Adjustments:
  Depreciation                      +25
  Deferred tax                      +5
  Increase in A/R                   -10
  Decrease in inventory             +5
  Increase in A/P                   +8
Net cash from operating activities  $133
```

**Key Rules (Indirect Method):**
- Decrease in non-cash current assets → add to NI
- Increase in non-cash current assets → subtract from NI
- Increase in current liabilities → add to NI
- Decrease in current liabilities → subtract from NI
- Non-cash expenses (depreciation) → add back
- Non-operating losses → add back; gains → subtract

**US GAAP vs IAS 7 Differences:**
- IAS 7 requires cash + cash equivalents; US GAAP permits cash only
- IAS 7 allows interest paid in operating OR financing; US GAAP requires operating
- US GAAP requires indirect method supplement if direct method used

### 1.4 Statement of Changes in Equity

**US GAAP:** Called "Statement of Retained Earnings"
```
Ending RE = Beginning RE − Dividends + Net Income
```

**IFRS (IAS 1.106) - Full Statement:**
Required columns for each equity component:
- Share Capital
- Share Premium
- Treasury Shares
- Retained Earnings
- AOCI (Foreign Exchange Reserve, Pension Reserve, Revaluation Reserve)
- Non-Controlling Interest

Reconciliation showing:
- Total comprehensive income (attributable to parent + NCI)
- Effects of retrospective application
- Transactions with owners (contributions, distributions)
- Changes in ownership that don't result in loss of control

## 2. Consolidation Reporting

### 2.1 Consolidated Financial Statements (IFRS 10 / IAS 27)

**Principle:** Present parent + subsidiaries as single economic entity.

**Complete Set (IAS 1.10):**
1. Consolidated statement of financial position
2. Consolidated statement of profit/loss and OCI
3. Consolidated statement of changes in equity
4. Consolidated statement of cash flows
5. Notes (accounting policies + explanatory info)
6. Operating segments report (IFRS 8)

**Two Core Procedures (IFRS 10.B86):**
1. Cancel out items that are asset in one company and liability in another
2. Add together all uncancelled items

**Eliminations Required:**
- "Investment in subsidiary" ↔ Subsidiary's share capital (IFRS 3.10)
- Intercompany payables ↔ receivables (IFRS 10.B86(c))
- Intercompany revenues ↔ expenses
- Unrealised profit in inventory (PURP)

### 2.2 Goodwill Calculation (IFRS 3.32)

```
Goodwill = Consideration Transferred (FV)
         + FV of Non-Controlling Interest (IFRS 3.19)
         − Ordinary Share Capital of Subsidiary
         − Share Premium of Subsidiary
         − Retained Earnings at Acquisition Date
         − Fair Value Adjustments at Acquisition
```

Negative goodwill → Recognized as "gain on bargain purchase" in P&L (IFRS 3.34).

### 2.3 Non-Controlling Interest (NCI)

**At Acquisition (IFRS 3.19):**
- Option A: Fair value (Full Goodwill method)
- Option B: Proportionate share of identifiable net assets

**Post-Acquisition (IFRS 10.B94):**
```
NCI Balance = NCI_Acq + (%NCI × ΔRE_post)
```
- Applied to profits/losses even if NCI goes to deficit

### 2.4 Intra-Group Elimination

**PURP Formula:**
```
PURP = Intercompany Profit × (Unsold Inventory / Total Intercompany Sales)
```
Required even for arm's-length transactions (group can't earn profit trading with itself).

### 2.5 Multi-Entity Comparison: Consolidated vs Separate

| Feature | Separate | Consolidated |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Perspective | Legal entity | Economic entity |
| Investments | Line item at cost | "Broken open" to show actual assets |
| Internal profits | Included | Eliminated |
| Liabilities | Parent only | Total group indebtedness |

## 3. Report Exchange Format: XBRL

### 3.1 Architecture

**XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language):**
- Global framework for exchanging business information
- Based on XML; now supports JSON and CSV formats
- Mandatory for SEC filings (US), HMRC/Companies House (UK), MCA (India)

**Document Structure:**
1. **XBRL Instance** - Contains business facts
   - Items: single-value facts with context reference
   - Tuples: multi-value facts
   - Contexts: entity + period + scenario
   - Units: currency (ISO 4217), shares, etc.

2. **XBRL Taxonomy** - Defines metadata
   - Schema: defines concepts (name, type, data type)
   - Five linkbases:
     - **Label**: Human-readable strings (multi-language)
     - **Reference**: Citations to authoritative literature
     - **Calculation**: Validation rules (summation weights)
     - **Definition**: Semantic relationships (is-a, whole-part, essence-alias)
     - **Presentation**: Hierarchical layout guidance

### 3.2 Inline XBRL (iXBRL)

- Embeds XBRL metadata in HTML documents
- Human-readable rendering in browser; machine-readable data underneath
- Required by UK HMRC, Companies House; accepted by SEC since 2016
- Solution for large statements: iXBRL document sets

### 3.3 Key Modules

| Module | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| XBRL Dimensions | Structured contextual info (dimensions, domains, hypercubes) |
| XBRL Formula | XPath expressions for validation and calculation |
| iXBRL | Embed XBRL in HTML/XHTML |
| XBRL Versioning | Document taxonomy changes across versions |
| XBRL Table Linkbase | Tabular reporting templates (Solvency II, COREP, FINREP) |

## 4. Report Generation Engine Architecture

### 4.1 Template-Based Design

**Core Components:**
- **Template Definition**: Layout structure + data bindings + calculation formulas
- **Data Provider Layer**: Abstracts ledger, journal entries, trial balance
- **Calculation Engine**: Executes formulas, handles currency, rounding, aggregations
- **Renderer**: Converts to PDF, Excel, HTML, XBRL, iXBRL
- **Scheduler**: Auto-generation with cron-like triggers + delivery (email, API, storage)

**Template Structure (conceptual):**
```yaml
report:
  id: balance_sheet_ifrs
  standard: IFRS
  sections:
    - name: Non-Current Assets
      type: section
      items:
        - code: PPE_NET
          label: "Property, Plant and Equipment"
          binding: account_group(1500-1599)
          calculation: SUM(accounts) - ACCUM_DEPR
        - code: GOODWILL
          label: "Goodwill"
          binding: account_group(1600)
    - name: Current Assets
      items:
        - code: CASH_EQUIV
          label: "Cash and Cash Equivalents"
          binding: account_group(1100-1199)
  calculations:
    - target: TOTAL_ASSETS
      formula: SUM(NON_CURRENT_ASSETS, CURRENT_ASSETS)
  validations:
    - TOTAL_ASSETS == TOTAL_LIABILITIES_AND_EQUITY
```

### 4.2 Parameterized Reports

**Filter Dimensions:**
- Period: fiscal year, quarter, month, custom date range
- Entity: single company, business unit, department
- Currency: functional, presentation, reporting
- Accounting standard: IFRS, US GAAP, local GAAP
- Comparison: actual, budget, forecast, prior period

### 4.3 Drill-Down Capability

**Path:** Summary → Line Item → Account Group → Ledger Account → Journal Entry → Source Document

Implementation:
- Each report cell carries a drill-path metadata (account_ids, period, entity)
- Click triggers a detail query with the same filter context
- Journal entry view shows: date, description, debit, credit, counter-entry, attachments

### 4.4 Comparative Reporting

| Type | Implementation |
|------|---------------|
| Month-over-Month | Side-by-side columns, period N vs period N-1 |
| Year-over-Year | Same month across years, growth % calculation |
| Budget vs Actual | Actual column + budget column + variance + variance % |
| Multi-Entity | Entity columns with consolidated total |
| Rolling | Trailing 12 months, quarterly rolling |

### 4.5 Export & Distribution

| Format | Use Case | Implementation |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| PDF | Archival, audit, presentation | Server-side rendering (wkhtmltopdf, Puppeteer) |
| Excel | Ad-hoc analysis, client delivery | openpyxl, xlsxwriter with formatting preservation |
| XBRL/iXBRL | Regulatory filings (SEC, HMRC, CVM) | Taxonomy-aware tagging engine |
| CSV | Data interchange, BI tools | Direct export with encoding control |
| API (JSON) | Programmatic access, dashboards | REST/GraphQL endpoints with pagination |
| Embedded | Customer-facing portals | iframe/JS widget with auth token |

### 4.6 White-Label / Customization

**Customization Points:**
- **Custom Fields**: User-defined attributes on accounts, entities, transactions
- **Custom Calculations**: User-defined formulas (e.g., custom KPIs, ratios)
- **Custom Layouts**: Drag-and-drop report builder with section ordering
- **Branding**: Logo, colors, fonts, header/footer text per tenant
- **Localization**: Multi-language labels, date/currency formatting per locale

## 7. Implementation Recommendations

### 7.1 Report Engine Architecture

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 Report Engine                     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Template Store    │  Data Provider Layer         │
│  (YAML/JSON defs)  │  (GL, Journal, TB, Budget)  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Calculation Engine │  Validation Engine           │
│  (formulas, agg)    │  (balance checks, rules)    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Renderer Pipeline:                               │
│  PDF ← HTML ← Data + Template                    │
│  Excel ← Data + Template                          │
│  XBRL ← Tagged Data + Taxonomy                    │
│  API ← JSON serialization                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Scheduler        │  Distribution                 │
│  (cron, event)    │  (email, S3, webhook, API)    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 7.2 Data Model (Key Entities)

```
ReportTemplate
  ├── id, name, standard (IFRS/GAAP/local)
  ├── sections[] (section_type, ordering, level)
  ├── items[] (code, label, account_binding, formula)
  ├── validations[] (target, expected, error_message)
  └── parameters[] (name, type, default, required)

ReportInstance
  ├── id, template_id, period, entity, currency
  ├── status (draft, generated, approved, published)
  ├── generated_at, generated_by
  └── output_files[] (format, path, checksum)

DrillPath
  ├── report_instance_id, item_code
  ├── depth, target_type (summary/detail/journal/source)
  └── query_params (filters applied)
```

### 7.3 Consolidation Engine

```
ConsolidationGroup
  ├── parent_entity_id
  ├── subsidiaries[] (entity_id, ownership_pct, acquisition_date)
  ├── elimination_rules[] (intercompany, PURP, NCI)
  └── currency_translation (method, rates_source)

ConsolidationRun
  ├── group_id, period, status
  ├── steps: collect → translate → eliminate → aggregate → validate → output
  └── output: consolidated_statements[]
```
