# F32: Treasury Management and Cash Operations for Financial Platforms

## Research Date: 2026-07-09

## Executive Summary
Research into treasury management systems reveals a modular architecture with distinct operational domains. The findings cover cash management, bank connectivity, payment operations, investment tracking, debt management, FX operations, cash flow categorization, reconciliation, and intercompany treasury. Each domain requires specific data models, calculation formulas, and integration patterns for a robust cashflow platform.

---

## 1. Cash Management: Daily Position and Forecasting

### 1.1 Daily Cash Position Tracking
- **Data Model**: BankAccount(id, name, bank_code, currency, balance, available_balance, last_sync)
- **Integration Pattern**: Real-time balance polling via Open Banking APIs (Pix in Brazil), batch reconciliation for legacy banks
- **Key Metrics**: Available balance vs. book balance, float analysis (D-0, D+1, D+2)

### 1.2 Cash Forecasting (7-30 Days)
- **Forecasting Methods**:
  - **Direct Method**: Cash receipts - cash disbursements = net cash flow
  - **Indirect Method**: Net income + adjustments for non-cash items + working capital changes
- **Formula**: `Forecasted Position = Current Balance + Σ(Expected Inflows) - Σ(Expected Outflows)`
- **Accuracy Metric**: Forecast Error % = |Actual - Forecast| / Actual × 100
- **Best Practice**: Rolling 13-week forecast updated weekly (AFP recommendation)

### 1.3 Cash Pooling
- **Zero Balancing**: Physical movement of funds to master account; intercompany loans created automatically
- **Notional Pooling**: Offsetting balances across accounts for interest calculation without physical movement
- **Data Model**: CashPool(id, name, pool_type[zero/notional], master_account_id, member_account_ids, currency, threshold)

## 2. Bank Account Management

### 2.1 Multi-Bank Connectivity
- **Integration Layer**: Bank Connectivity Hub with adapters per bank
- **Protocol**: ISO 20022 XML (CAMT.052 for balance reports, CAMT.053 for statements)
- **Brazil-Specific**: Open Finance Brasil APIs (Pix, account aggregation)

### 2.2 Open Finance Brasil Integration
- **API Endpoints**:
  - `/accounts/{accountId}/balances` - Real-time balance
  - `/accounts/{accountId}/transactions` - Transaction history
  - `/consents` - Data sharing consent management
- **Data Model**: BankConsent(id, user_id, bank_id, consent_id, status, granted_at, expires_at)
- **Security**: OAuth 2.0 + mTLS, Brazilian Central Bank (BCB) certification required

### 2.3 Balance Inquiry & Transaction History
- **Real-time**: Push notifications via webhooks for balance changes
- **Batch**: Daily SFTP for transaction history reconciliation
- **Normalization**: Map bank-specific transaction codes to platform categories

## 3. Payment Scheduling

### 3.1 Batch Payments
- **Data Model**: PaymentBatch(id, created_by, status, total_amount, currency, payments[])
- **Payment**: Payment(id, batch_id, payee_name, payee_account, amount, currency, scheduled_date, status)
- **File Formats**: CNAB 240 (Brazilian standard), ISO 20022 pain.001

### 3.2 Payment Approval Workflows
- **Workflow Stages**:
  1. Creation → 2. Validation → 3. Approval (1 or 2 levels) → 4. Execution → 5. Confirmation
- **Threshold Rules**: Amount > X requires dual approval; Amount > Y requires CFO approval
- **Data Model**: ApprovalRule(id, min_amount, max_amount, required_approvals, approvers[])

### 3.3 Payment Date Optimization (Float Management)
- **Strategy**: Optimize payment timing to maximize interest income/ minimize interest expense
- **Calculation**: `Optimal Date = Due Date - Bank Processing Days - Safety Buffer`
- **Brazil Context**: Consider SELIC rate for overnight investments, D-1 for CDI rates

## 4. Investment Tracking

### 4.1 Money Market Funds
- **Data Model**: Investment(id, type, instrument_code, purchase_date, maturity_date, principal, current_value, yield)
- **Types**: CDB (Certificado de Depósito Bancário), LCI, LCA, Fundos Renda Fixa

### 4.2 Treasury Bills & CDIs
- **Brazilian Treasury**: Tesouro Direto (Tesouro Selic, Tesouro IPCA+, Tesouro Prefixado)
- **CDI (Certificado de Depósito Interbancário)**: Reference rate for Brazilian fixed income
- **Data Model**: FixedIncomeInstrument(id, ticker, issue_date, maturity_date, rate_type[pre/post], rate_value, face_value)

### 4.3 Yield Calculation
- **Pre-fixed**: `Yield = Principal × (1 + rate)^days/252 - Principal`
- **Post-fixed (CDI)**: `Yield = Principal × (1 + CDI_daily)^days - Principal`
- **IPCA+**: `Yield = Principal × (1 + IPCA_daily × (1 + fixed_rate))^days - Principal`
- **Day Count Convention**: Brazilian market uses 252 business days/year

## 5. Debt Management

### 5.1 Loan Tracking
- **Data Model**: Loan(id, lender, principal, interest_rate, start_date, maturity_date, amortization_type[SAC/Price], status)
- **Integration**: Bank API for loan balance, covenant compliance reports

### 5.2 Amortization Schedules
- **SAC (Sistema de Amortização Constante)**:
  - `Amortization = Principal / Total_Periods`
  - `Interest = Outstanding_Balance × Rate`
  - `Payment = Amortization + Interest`
- **Price (Tabela Price)**:
  - `Payment = Principal × [rate × (1+rate)^n] / [(1+rate)^n - 1]`
  - `Interest = Outstanding_Balance × Rate`
  - `Amortization = Payment - Interest`

### 5.3 Covenants Monitoring
- **Key Ratios**: DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio), Leverage Ratio, Current Ratio
- **Data Model**: Covenant(id, loan_id, metric_name, threshold_value, threshold_type[min/max], actual_value, status)
- **Alert System**: Real-time monitoring with escalation when approaching thresholds

## 6. FX Operations

### 6.1 Currency Exchange
- **Data Model**: FXTransaction(id, from_currency, to_currency, amount, exchange_rate, value_date, settlement_date)
- **Rate Sources**: BCB (Brazilian Central Bank), Reuters, Bloomberg
- **Integration**: SWIFT for international transfers, local payment systems for domestic

### 6.2 Forward Contracts
- **Data Model**: FXForward(id, buy_currency, sell_currency, forward_rate, value_date, settlement_date, notional_amount, counterparty)
- **Pricing**: `Forward Rate = Spot Rate × (1 + domestic_rate × days/360) / (1 + foreign_rate × days/360)`

### 6.3 Hedge Accounting (IFRS 9 / ASC 815)
- **Cash Flow Hedge**: Hedge derivative gains/losses go to OCI until hedged item affects P&L
- **Fair Value Hedge**: Hedge gains/losses recognized in P&L immediately
- **Documentation**: Hedge relationship, effectiveness testing (80-125% band)

### 6.4 FX Gain/Loss Calculation
- **Formula**: `FX_GainLoss = (Ending_Rate - Beginning_Rate) × Foreign_Currency_Balance`
- **Translation**: For consolidated financials, use closing rate for balance sheet, average rate for P&L

## 7. Cash Flow Categorization

### 7.1 Operating Activities
- **Inflows**: Customer receipts, interest received, dividends received
- **Outflows**: Supplier payments, salaries, rent, taxes, interest paid
- **Formula**: `Net_Operating_Cash_Flow = Cash_Receipts_from_Customers - Cash_Paid_to_Suppliers_and_Employees`

### 7.2 Investing Activities
- **Inflows**: Sale of investments, disposal of fixed assets, loan repayments received
- **Outflows**: Purchase of investments, acquisition of fixed assets, loans given
- **Formula**: `Net_Investing_Cash_Flow = Proceeds_from_Disposal - Purchase_of_Assets_and_Investments`

### 7.3 Financing Activities
- **Inflows**: Proceeds from borrowings, capital contributions
- **Outflows**: Repayment of borrowings, dividends paid, share buybacks
- **Formula**: `Net_Financing_Cash_Flow = Proceeds_from_Borrowings - Repayment_of_Borrowings_and_Dividends`

### 7.4 IAS 7 / ASC 230 Requirements
- **Direct Method**: Preferred for operating activities (shows cash receipts and payments)
- **Indirect Method**: Reconciliation from net income to operating cash flow
- **Data Model**: CashFlowCategory(id, transaction_id, category[operating/investing/financing], subcategory, amount, date)

## 8. Bank Reconciliation Automation

### 8.1 Matching Rules
- **Exact Match**: Amount + Reference Number
- **Fuzzy Match**: Amount ± tolerance + Date range + Counterparty name similarity
- **Rule Engine**: Configurable matching rules with confidence scores

### 8.2 Auto-Reconciliation Thresholds
- **Threshold Strategy**: Auto-reconcile transactions with confidence > 95%
- **Data Model**: ReconciliationRule(id, match_criteria, tolerance_amount, tolerance_days, auto_reconcile_threshold)
- **Metrics**: Auto-reconciliation rate target: > 85%

### 8.3 Exception Handling
- **Exception Types**: Unmatched transactions, partial matches, duplicates
- **Workflow**: Exception → Investigation → Resolution → Learning (update rules)
- **Data Model**: ReconciliationException(id, transaction_id, type, status, assigned_to, resolution_notes)

## 9. Intercompany Treasury

### 9.1 Cash Transfers Between Entities
- **Data Model**: IntercompanyTransfer(id, from_entity, to_entity, amount, currency, purpose, fx_rate, status)
- **Compliance**: Transfer pricing documentation, thin capitalization rules

### 9.2 Netting
- **Bilateral Netting**: Offset payables/receivables between two entities
- **Multilateral Netting**: Central netting center calculates net positions
- **Formula**: `Net_Position = Σ(Receivables) - Σ(Payables)`

### 9.3 Intercompany Loans
- **Data Model**: IntercompanyLoan(id, lender_entity, borrower_entity, principal, interest_rate, start_date, maturity_date, currency)
- **Pricing**: Arm's length principle - use market reference rates (e.g., SOFR, CDI + spread)
- **Compliance**: Withholding tax implications, thin capitalization rules, transfer pricing documentation

## 10. Integration Architecture Patterns

### 10.1 Event-Driven Architecture
- **Events**: TransactionCreated, BalanceUpdated, PaymentInitiated, ReconciliationCompleted
- **Message Broker**: Apache Kafka or RabbitMQ for event streaming
- **Pattern**: CQRS for read/write separation in reporting vs. operational systems

### 10.2 API Design
- **REST**: Resource-oriented design for CRUD operations
- **GraphQL**: For complex reporting queries with nested relationships
- **gRPC**: For high-performance internal service communication

### 10.3 Data Storage
- **Operational Data**: PostgreSQL with JSONB for flexible attributes
- **Time-Series Data**: TimescaleDB for balance history, transaction history
- **Analytics**: ClickHouse or BigQuery for reporting and forecasting

### 10.4 Security & Compliance
- **Encryption**: TLS 1.3 in transit, AES-256 at rest
- **Access Control**: RBAC with least-privilege principle
- **Audit Trail**: Immutable audit logs for all financial transactions
- **Brazilian Compliance**: LGPD (data protection), BCB regulations, CVM rules

## 11. Performance Metrics

### 11.1 Working Capital Metrics (AFP)
- **Net Working Capital**: Current Assets - Current Liabilities
- **Current Ratio**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- **Quick Ratio**: (Cash + Receivables) / Current Liabilities
- **Days Cash Held**: Cash / (Operating Expenses - Non-cash Expenses) × 365
- **DSO**: (Average AR / Revenue) × 365
- **DPO**: (Average AP / COGS) × 365
- **DIO**: (Inventory / COGS) × 365

### 11.2 Treasury KPIs
- **Forecast Accuracy**: |Actual - Forecast| / Actual × 100 (target: < 10%)
- **Auto-Reconciliation Rate**: Auto-matched / Total transactions (target: > 85%)
- **Payment Success Rate**: Successful payments / Total payments (target: > 99%)
- **Cash Conversion Cycle**: DSO + DIO - DPO
