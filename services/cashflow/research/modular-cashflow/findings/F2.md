# F2: Industry-Specific Financial Modules

## Findings

### [1] Retail POS systems are tightly coupled with inventory management, requiring FIFO/LIFO costing, barcode-driven transactions, and real-time stock updates across multiple locations.
- quote: "POS terminal software may also include features for additional functionality, such as inventory management, CRM, financials, or warehousing."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point-of-sale
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

### [2] Retail POS must handle perishable goods tracking, customer credit storage, and multi-outlet stock transfers — features not present in generic invoicing systems.
- quote: "Some business's goods may include perishables and hence the inventory system must be capable of prompting the admin and cashier on expiring or expired products. Some retail businesses require the system to store credit for their customers, credit which can be used subsequently to pay for goods."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point-of-sale
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

### [3] Cloud-based POS for chain stores requires instant centralization of inventory data across outlets with HQ-level stock control, a requirement unique to multi-location retail.
- quote: "Another advantage of a cloud-based POS are instant centralization of data (important especially to chain stores), ability to access data from anywhere there is internet connection, and lower start-up costs."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Point-of-sale
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

### [4] MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) is the canonical SaaS metric, calculated as average monthly client payment multiplied by number of clients, excluding one-time implementation fees and hardware.
- quote: "MRR = average monthly client payment × number of clients. This number excludes all one-time, non-recurring payments; for instance, implementation or professional service fees, hardware, and discounts."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revenue_stream
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-01-01
- confidence: high

### [5] SaaS subscription models require churn tracking, expansion revenue (upsell/cross-sell), and tiered/usage-based pricing — financial structures with no analog in transaction-based industries.
- quote: "The tiered pricing model is frequently used in software as a service (SaaS) platforms, offering customers different access levels and features based on their subscription tier. Another approach is the usage-based pricing model, which calculates charges based on the extent of service or product utilization by the customer."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subscription_business_model
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-05
- confidence: high

### [6] Manufacturing COGS requires three-component cost allocation (material + labor + allocated overhead) with variance tracking against standard costs — fundamentally different from retail resale costing.
- quote: "The cost of goods produced in the business should include all costs of production. The key components of cost generally include: Parts, raw materials and supplies used; Labor, including associated costs such as payroll taxes and benefits; and Overhead of the business allocated to production."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cost_of_goods_sold
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-04-03
- confidence: high

### [7] Manufacturing BOMs are hierarchical parent-child structures with configurable variants — the financial system must roll up costs through multi-level assemblies to compute unit economics.
- quote: "A bill of materials or product structure is a list of the raw materials, sub-assemblies, intermediate assemblies, sub-components, parts, and the quantities of each needed to manufacture an end product. A configurable bill of materials (CBOM) is a form of BOM used by industries that have multiple options and highly configurable products."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_of_materials
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-07-06
- confidence: high

### [8] Marketplace escrow systems hold buyer funds in a third-party account until transaction conditions are verified, requiring condition-based disbursement logic not found in standard invoicing.
- quote: "An escrow is a contractual arrangement in which a third party (the stakeholder or escrow agent) receives and disburses money or property for the primary transacting parties, with the disbursement dependent on conditions agreed to by the transacting parties."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escrow
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-28
- confidence: high

### [9] Real estate property management requires rent collection with security deposit tracking, late-fee enforcement, and lease-term-aware billing cycles — a distinct financial module from general accounts receivable.
- quote: "Managers are responsible for collecting rent, enforcing payment terms, issuing late notices, and managing security deposits. Many also prepare financial statements, budgets, and reports for property owners, ensuring compliance with local and national regulations."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_management
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-11
- confidence: high

### [10] Real estate property management fee structures include percentage-of-rent (8–12%), flat-fee, hybrid, and guaranteed-rent models — each requiring different financial settlement logic.
- quote: "The percentage of rent model is the most common property management model... The management company usually retains between 8% and 12% of the rental income as a management fee, remitting the remainder to the property owner."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Property_management
- source_type: primary
- published: 2026-06-11
- confidence: high

### [11] Healthcare insurance claims require a multi-step adjudication pipeline: claim submission → adjuster investigation → coverage verification → payment authorization → subrogation, with fraud detection and leak detection as mandatory controls.
- quote: "Claims and loss handling is the materialized utility of insurance; it is the actual 'product' paid for... The adjuster undertakes an investigation of each claim, usually in close cooperation with the insured, determines if coverage is available under the terms of the insurance contract."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insurance
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high

### [12] Agriculture crop insurance uses index-based triggers tied to climate data (rainfall, temperature thresholds) rather than individual loss assessment — requiring integration with external data feeds for automated payout determination.
- quote: "Index-based insurance uses models of how climate extremes affect crop production to define certain climate triggers that if surpassed have high probabilities of causing substantial crop loss. When harvest losses occur associated with exceeding the climate trigger threshold, the index-insured farmer is entitled to a compensation payment."
- url: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insurance
- source_type: primary
- published: unknown
- confidence: high
