## 1. GHG Protocol — Carbon Accounting Framework

**Source**: GHG Protocol Corporate Standard (ghgprotocol.org)
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source, WRI/WBCSD standard)

The GHG Protocol Corporate Accounting and Reporting Standard provides the foundational framework for corporate-level GHG emissions inventory. Covers seven Kyoto Protocol gases: CO2, CH4, N2O, HFCs, PFCs, SF6, NF3.

### Emission Scopes

- **Scope 1**: Direct emissions from owned/controlled sources (combustion, process, mobile sources, fugitive emissions).
- **Scope 2**: Indirect emissions from purchased/acquired electricity, steam, heat, and cooling. Three methods: location-based, market-based, and residual mix.
- **Scope 3**: Value chain emissions across 15 categories:
  1. Purchased goods and services
  2. Capital goods
  3. Fuel- and energy-related activities (not in Scope 1/2)
  4. Upstream transportation and distribution
  5. Waste generated in operations
  6. Business travel
  7. Employee commuting
  8. Upstream leased assets
  9. Downstream transportation and distribution
  10. Processing of sold products
  11. Use of sold products
  12. End-of-life treatment of sold products
  13. Downstream leased assets
  14. Franchises
  15. Investments

### Calculation Methods

- **Emissions = Activity Data × Emission Factor**
- Activity data: quantity of fuel consumed, electricity purchased, distance traveled
- Emission factors: sourced from IPCC, EPA, DEFRA, ecoinvent, or proprietary databases
- Global Warming Potential (GWP) values convert gas-specific emissions to CO2-equivalent (CO2e)

### Key Metrics for Financial Platforms

- Total GHG emissions (tCO2e) by scope
- Carbon intensity per revenue unit
- Carbon intensity per employee
- Year-over-year absolute change
- Scope 1+2 emissions (baseline requirement)
- Scope 3 categories material to the business

## 2. IFRS S1 — General Sustainability Disclosure Requirements

**Source**: IFRS Foundation (ifrs.org)
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source, issued June 2023)

IFRS S1 requires entities to disclose sustainability-related risks and opportunities useful to investors for resource-allocation decisions. Effective for annual periods beginning on or after 1 January 2024.

### Core Disclosure Pillars (4-pillar structure)

1. **Governance**: Processes, controls, and procedures to monitor/manage sustainability-related risks and opportunities
2. **Strategy**: How the entity manages sustainability-related risks and opportunities (short/medium/long term)
3. **Risk Management**: Processes to identify, assess, prioritize, and monitor risks; integration with overall risk management
4. **Metrics and Targets**: Performance against sustainability targets, including regulatory-mandated targets

### Key Requirements

- Disclosures about risks/opportunities affecting cash flows, access to finance, or cost of capital
- Connected information between sustainability disclosures and financial statements
- Scenario analysis for climate-related risks (referenced in IFRS S2)
- Use of industry-based disclosures from SASB Standards as a source of guidance

## 3. IFRS S2 — Climate-related Disclosures

**Source**: IFRS Foundation (ifrs.org)
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source, issued June 2023, amended December 2025)

IFRS S2 integrates TCFD recommendations with SASB industry-based requirements. Requires disclosure of climate-related physical and transition risks.

### Scope

- **Physical risks**: Acute (extreme weather) and chronic (sea level rise, temperature shifts) climate risks
- **Transition risks**: Policy/legal, technology, market, and reputational risks from transition to a low-carbon economy
- **Climate-related opportunities**: Resource efficiency, energy sources, products/services, markets, resilience

### GHG Emissions Requirements

- Mandatory Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions disclosure
- December 2025 amendments reduced implementation complexity while preserving investor usefulness
- Location-based and market-based methods for Scope 2 required
- Scope 3 disclosures required if material (with transition relief during implementation)

### Financial Integration

- Climate scenario analysis (including 1.5°C aligned scenarios)
- Internal carbon pricing disclosure
- Transition plan disclosures
- Climate-related financial metrics: climate-related capital expenditure, climate-related revenue, climate-related operating costs

## 4. SASB Standards — Industry-Based Sustainability Disclosures

**Source**: IFRS Foundation / SASB (ifrs.org/issued-standards/sasb-standards)
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source)

SASB Standards identify sustainability-related risks and opportunities material to investors across 77 industries in 11 sectors.

### Structure

- Industry-specific metrics and disclosure topics
- Evidence-based, market-informed standard-setting process
- Maintained by ISSB as part of IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards
- SASB Standards Taxonomy available for digital reporting

### 11 Industry Sectors

1. Commercial & Professional Services
2. Consumer Goods
3. Financials
4. Health Care
5. Infrastructure
6. Materials & Resources
7. Oil & Gas
8. Resource Transformation
9. Services
10. Technology & Communications
11. Transportation

### Financial Services Sector Metrics

- **Financial Services — Banking**: Systemic risks, products & services, business model resilience, environmental footprint, human capital, community relations
- **Financial Services — Insurance**: Climate change adaptation, product design, environmental footprint, human capital
- **Financial Services — Asset Management & Securities Brokerage**: ESG integration, systemic risks, human capital

## 5. EU CSRD — Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

**Source**: EUR-Lex, Directive (EU) 2022/2464
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary legal text)

### Scope

- All large undertakings (meeting 2 of 3 criteria: >250 employees, >EUR 40M turnover, >EUR 20M total assets)
- All undertakings (except micro) whose securities are admitted to trading on an EU regulated market
- Parent undertakings of large groups
- Third-country undertakings with >EUR 150M net turnover in the EU and a subsidiary/branch

### Double Materiality

- **Impact materiality**: How the undertaking's activities impact people and the environment
- **Financial materiality**: How sustainability matters create financial risks/opportunities for the undertaking
- Both dimensions must be assessed and disclosed simultaneously

### European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)

- Developed by EFRAG and adopted by the Commission via delegated acts
- Cover: environment (climate change, pollution, water, biodiversity, resource use), social (workforce, value chain workers, communities, consumers), governance (business conduct)
- Digital taxonomy for machine-readable reporting (XBRL/iXBRL)
- Phased implementation: largest public-interest entities first (FY 2024 reports), then other large undertakings (FY 2025), then listed SMEs (FY 2026 with opt-out until 2028)

### Assurance Requirements

- Limited assurance initially, transitioning to reasonable assurance
- Must be provided by an authorized statutory auditor or independent assurance services provider

## 6. GRI Standards — Impact Reporting

**Source**: Global Reporting Initiative (globalreporting.org)
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source)

The GRI Standards are the most widely used standards globally for sustainability impact reporting. Modular structure allows standalone or integrated ESG reports.

### Three-Tier Structure

1. **Universal Standards** (GRI 1, 2, 3):
   - GRI 1: Foundation — reporting principles, due diligence, reporting practices
   - GRI 2: General Disclosures — organizational details, governance, strategy, stakeholder engagement
   - GRI 3: Material Topics — process for determining material topics

2. **Sector Standards**: Industry-specific material topics and disclosures (e.g., GRI 11: Oil & Gas, GRI 12: Coal, GRI 13: Agriculture, Aquaculture & Fishing Sectors)

3. **Topic Standards**: Disclosure requirements organized by topic:
   - Environmental: GRI 301-308 (Materials, Energy, Water, Biodiversity, Emissions, Waste, Transport, Supplier Assessment)
   - Social: GRI 401-418 (Employment, OHS, Training, Diversity, Human Rights, Local Communities, Public Policy, Customer Health & Safety, Marketing, Customer Privacy)
   - Economic: GRI 201-207 (Performance, Market Presence, Procurement, Anti-corruption, Anti-competitive Behavior, Tax)

### Key Disclosures for Financial Platforms

- GRI 305: Emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3, GHG reduction goals)
- GRI 302: Energy (consumption, energy mix, efficiency)
- GRI 308: Supplier Environmental Assessment
- GRI 203: Indirect Economic Impacts
- GRI 205: Anti-corruption
- GRI 418: Customer Privacy

### GRI Sustainability Taxonomy

- Digital taxonomy for machine-readable reporting
- Maps GRI disclosures to XBRL taxonomy elements
- Enables automated data aggregation and comparison

## 7. EU Taxonomy Regulation — Green Finance Classification

**Source**: EU Regulation 2020/852 (referenced in CSRD text)
**Confidence**: MEDIUM (cross-reference from CSRD legal text)

The EU Taxonomy creates a classification system for environmentally sustainable economic activities.

### Six Environmental Objectives

1. Climate change mitigation
2. Climate change adaptation
3. Sustainable use and protection of water and marine resources
4. Transition to a circular economy
5. Pollution prevention and control
6. Protection and restoration of biodiversity and ecosystems

### Technical Screening Criteria

- Activities must substantially contribute to at least one objective
- Must do no significant harm (DNSH) to any other objective
- Must comply with minimum social safeguards
- Activity-specific thresholds set by Commission delegated acts

### Financial Platform Requirements

- **Article 8 of CSRD**: Large undertakings must disclose the proportion of taxonomy-aligned turnover, CapEx, and OpEx
- Taxonomy alignment percentage as a key sustainability KPI
- Non-financial market participants must disclose taxonomy alignment of their portfolios (SFDR)

## 8. Green Bonds & Sustainability-Linked Finance

**Source**: Multiple (Climate Bonds Initiative, ICMA Green Bond Principles)
**Confidence**: MEDIUM (secondary sources, widely established standards)

### Green Bonds

- **ICMA Green Bond Principles (GBP)**: Voluntary guidelines for use of proceeds (renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean transport, green buildings, etc.)
- Proceeds must be tracked via a Green Bond Register
- External review/verification required (second-party opinion, verification, certification, or rating)
- EU Green Bond Standard (EU GBS): Voluntary standard aligned with EU Taxonomy; requires 100% taxonomy-aligned allocation of proceeds

### Sustainability-Linked Loans (SLLs)

- **ICMA Sustainability-Linked Loan Principles (SLLP)**: KPI-linked financing
- Pricing ratchet: interest rate adjusts based on borrower's performance against pre-defined Sustainability Performance Targets (SPTs)
- Key components: KPI, SPT, loan characteristics, reporting, verification

### ESG Scoring for Investments

- **MSCI ESG Ratings**: AAA to CCC scale; industry-relative
- **Sustainalytics**: Risk rating (quantitative assessment of unmanaged ESG risk)
- **ISS ESG**: Quality ratings and norm-based screening
- **S&P Global ESG Scores**: Based on CSA (Corporate Sustainability Assessment)

## 9. Supply Chain Sustainability — Scope 3 Deep Dive

**Source**: GHG Protocol Scope 3 Calculation Guidance
**Confidence**: HIGH (primary source)

### Scope 3 Categories Relevant to Financial Services

| Category | Description | Calculation Approach |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Category 1 | Purchased goods & services | Spend-based or average-data method |
| Category 2 | Capital goods | Spend-based method using economic input-output LCA |
| Category 4 | Upstream transportation | Freight ton-km × emission factor |
| Category 7 | Employee commuting | Survey-based or distance-based |
| Category 11 | Use of sold products | For financial services: financed emissions |
| Category 15 | Investments | PCAF methodology for financed emissions |

### PCAF Methodology for Financed Emissions

- **Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials (PCAF)**: Standard for measuring Scope 3 Category 15 (Investments)
- **Emission = Exposure × Emission Factor**
  - Exposure: share of equity/debt held
  - Emission factor: tCO2e per enterprise value (EVIC) or tCO2e per revenue
- Data quality scoring: 1 (actual) to 5 (estimated)
- Phase 1: Listed equity and corporate bonds
- Phase 2: Business loans and non-listed equity
- Phase 3: Project finance and commercial real estate
- Phase 4: Sovereign debt
- Phase 5: Mortgages

### Data Collection Methods (Ranked by Quality)

1. **Supplier-specific data**: Direct measurement from suppliers (highest quality)
2. **Hybrid method**: Combine primary activity data with secondary emission factors
3. **Average-data method**: Use industry averages or country-specific factors
4. **Spend-based method**: Economic activity (revenue/spend) × economic input-output emission factor (lowest quality)

## 10. ESG Metrics Integration with Financial Statements

**Source**: IFRS Foundation connectivity guidance, SASB materiality map
**Confidence**: MEDIUM (cross-framework synthesis)

### Connected Information Requirements

IFRS S1 and IFRS S2 require "connected information" between sustainability disclosures and financial statements. This means:

- Sustainability risks must be reflected in financial statement assumptions (e.g., impairment, fair value)
- Climate-related capital expenditure disclosed alongside IAS 16 property, plant & equipment
- Carbon-related provisions (e.g., EU ETS liability) disclosed alongside IAS 37 provisions
- Transition-related costs linked to income statement categories

### Key Financial-ESG Linkages

| ESG Metric | Financial Statement Linkage | Framework |
|------------|----------------------------|-----------|
| GHG emissions (Scope 1) | Cost of purchased fuels, carbon allowance costs | GHG Protocol, IFRS S2 |
| GHG emissions (Scope 2) | Electricity/steam costs | GHG Protocol, IFRS S2 |
| Carbon credits/trading | Revenue/cost, fair value of allowances | IFRS S2, IAS 38 |
| Climate-related CapEx | Capital expenditure plans, asset additions | IFRS S2, IAS 16 |
| Stranded asset risk | Impairment testing (IAS 36), fair value (IFRS 13) | IFRS S2 |
| Biodiversity impact | Asset impairment, provisions for remediation | GRI 304, ISSB nature project |
| Water consumption | Operating costs, regulatory provisions | GRI 303 |

### Natural Capital Accounting

- **System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA)**: UN statistical framework integrating environmental data with national accounts
- **Natural Capital Protocol**: Business-focused framework for measuring and valuing natural capital impacts and dependencies
- **Capitals Coalition**: Merged Natural Capital Protocol and Social & Human Capital Protocol into integrated multi-capital approach

## 11. ESG Reporting Automation & Dashboard Architecture

**Source**: Framework synthesis (IFRS taxonomy, GRI taxonomy, ESRS digital taxonomy)
**Confidence**: MEDIUM (architectural guidance based on standard requirements)

### Data Collection Architecture

1. **Internal Data Sources**:
   - ERP systems (energy consumption, procurement, waste)
   - HR systems (employee demographics, training, turnover)
   - Travel booking systems (business travel emissions)
   - Fleet management (vehicle emissions)
   - Building management (facilities energy, water)

2. **External Data Sources**:
   - Grid electricity emission factors (by region, utility)
   - Supplier ESG data (via CDP Supply Chain, EcoVadis, Sedex)
   - Market data (enterprise value for PCAF calculations)
   - Regulatory carbon pricing (EU ETS, national schemes)

3. **Emission Factor Databases**:
   - IPCC Emission Factor Database (EFDB)
   - DEFRA/DESNZ conversion factors (UK)
   - EPA Emission Factors Hub (US)
   - Ecoinvent (life cycle inventory)
   - Ecoinvent/GHG Protocol sector-specific factors

### Digital Reporting Taxonomies

- **IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Taxonomy**: XBRL taxonomy for ISSB standards
- **GRI Sustainability Taxonomy**: Machine-readable GRI disclosures
- **ESRS Digital Taxonomy**: iXBRL for EU CSRD compliance
- **SASB Standards Taxonomy**: Industry-specific digital tags

### Automated Filing Pipeline

```
Data Collection → Validation → Emission Calculation → 
Consolidation → Materiality Assessment → Disclosure Generation → 
Tagging (XBRL/iXBRL) → Assurance → Publication
```

## 14. Implementation Recommendations for Cashflow Platform

### Phase 1: Foundation (Scope 1+2)

- Emission calculation engine using GHG Protocol formula (Activity × Factor)
- Emission factor database integration (DEFRA/IPCC as defaults, customizable)
- Basic dashboard: total emissions, intensity metrics, YoY trends
- IFRS S2 compliant disclosure templates

### Phase 2: Value Chain (Scope 3)

- PCAF methodology integration for financed emissions
- Supplier data collection pipeline (spend-based → hybrid → primary data)
- Supply chain ESG assessment module (EcoVadis/Sedex integration)
- Data quality scoring and uncertainty tracking

### Phase 3: Regulatory Compliance

- EU CSRD/ESRS disclosure generation with double materiality assessment
- XBRL/iXBRL tagging for digital filing
- GRI Standards disclosure mapping
- SASB industry-specific metric tracking

### Phase 4: Green Finance

- Green bond allocation tracking (taxonomy-aligned CapEx/OpEx)
- Sustainability-linked loan KPI monitoring
- ESG scoring integration (MSCI/Sustainalytics APIs)
- Carbon credit/trading position tracking
