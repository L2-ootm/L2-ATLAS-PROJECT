### WR-01: `AlreadyDecided.decision` can mismatch the winning action in a mixed approve/reject race

**Files modified:** `services/agent-runtime/atlas_runtime/permission_broker.py`
**Commit:** 5e214e2
**Status:** fixed: requires human verification (concurrency/logic fix)

**Applied fix:** Used REVIEW option (b), as directed by the fix prompt — derive the
loser's `AlreadyDecided.decision` from the authoritative WINNING terminal row
status (the value the WR-04 bounded poll converges on) instead of reading the
race-prone, last-writer-wins `decision` column.

In the lost-race branch of `claim()` (the `except ToolApprovalError` handler),
after the bounded poll reaches a terminal status:
- `status == 'rejected'` -> `decision = 'reject'`, and `reason = current.reason`
  (authoritative: `tool_service.reject()` persists the winning reject reason onto
  the row).
- `status in ('executed', 'failed')` -> `decision = 'approve'`, and `reason = None`
  (`approve()` writes no `reason`; the persisted reason is stale/unset for an
  approve winner, so it is not surfaced as the winning outcome). `failed` is
  `approve()`'s terminal when the authorized tool itself errored, so it remains an
  approve decision.

**Invariant preservation (per fix-prompt constraints):**
- No status-flip UPDATE was reintroduced into `permission_broker.claim()`. The
  pre-flip `decision`-column write (lines 581-585) remains a non-status update; the
  atomic at-most-once guard (`rowcount==1`) stays solely in
  `tool_service.approve()`/`reject()`.
- The loser's `AlreadyDecided` now carries the WINNING outcome derived from the
  converged terminal status, eliminating the `executed`+`reject` mismatch.
- IN-01 (negative `LIMIT`) and IN-02 (`created_at` ISO coercion) were left alone —
  Info severity, out of `critical_warning` scope.
